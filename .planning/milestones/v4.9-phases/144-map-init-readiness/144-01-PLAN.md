<objective>
Retire the store-and-poll legacy-taxon dance in `<bee-atlas>` and consolidate the two
separate "are we mid-resolve" signals into one gate, WITHOUT changing observable behavior.

1. Legacy-taxon URL resolution becomes one linear `await taxaReady` path (ready.ts), replacing
   the `_pendingLegacyTaxon` store-in-firstUpdated → re-check-in-`_loadSummaryFromSQLite` →
   re-store-in-`_resolveLegacyTaxon` cycle.
2. A dedicated `_filterResolving` boolean feeds a single `intendedFilterActive` getter
   (`isFilterActive(this._filterState) || this._filterResolving`). The firstUpdated hide-all
   guard AND the `_replaceUrlState`/`_pushUrlStateDebounced` URL-write suppression both read
   from this one getter — eliminating the second source of truth (`_pendingLegacyTaxon != null`).

Purpose: kill the recurring map-init race class structurally at its bee-atlas source — the legacy
strand and the pending-resolve gate stop depending on `_loadSummaryFromSQLite` ordering.
Output: a behavior-identical `<bee-atlas>` whose legacy resolution is await-based and whose
hide-all + URL-suppression read one gate. The render-decision move into `<bee-map>` is Plan 02.

NOTE: This plan KEEPS the existing empty-collection pre-seeding in firstUpdated/_onPopState
(`_visibleIds = new Set()`, `_filteredGeoJSON = { ...features: [] }`). Removing that pre-seed
is Plan 02's job (render = f(intent) in bee-map). Do not remove it here, or an unfiltered flash
re-opens between the two plans.
</objective>
