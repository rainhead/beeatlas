## Deviations from Plan

### Honest Finding: .git Reclaim Was ~1 MB, Not ~150 MB

**Found during:** Task 3 (operator filter-repo execution)

**Issue:** D-01 in the PLAN estimated a ~150 MB `.git` size reduction. The actual reclaim was approximately 1 MB (36M → 35M).

**Explanation:** Git's packfile delta-compression had already made the repeated large JSON blobs cheap to store in pack format. The absolute disk size of the uncompressed working-tree files (collectors.json 3.5 MB + collector_event_pages.json 29 MB = ~32.5 MB) would have predicted a large raw reclaim, but the pack compression eliminated most of that overhead from day one. The history-cleanliness goal (no artifact in any future checkout, no future re-commit bloat risk) is fully met regardless of the disk savings being smaller than anticipated.

**Impact on plan:** The primary goals — removing artifacts from git tracking (D-02) and purging them from history (D-01, D-07) — are completely achieved. The ROADMAP success criterion notes ~150 MB reclaim; this should be read as an order-of-magnitude estimate that did not account for pack compression. The security/hygiene goal is satisfied.

No auto-fix was applicable (this is an observation about a completed operator step, not a bug or missing feature).
