## Core Goals

1. **Tighten learning cycles** — close the gap between "I collected this" and "it appears identified on the map." Provisional iNat specimen records before Ecdysis ingestion are the most direct expression of this.

2. **Convey liveness and togetherness** — volunteers should feel they're part of something active and shared, not depositing data into a void.

## Cold-Start Strategy

Deliver value to first users by combining and surfacing existing data in ways that are difficult to achieve without the site. Don't build community features until there are people using the site semi-regularly — avoid the empty community platform problem.

In the meantime, surface community activity from external platforms (iNat comments, determination feeds) to convey presence without requiring on-site participation.

## Long-Term: BeeAtlas as the Gathering Place

The alternatives all fall short:
- **Canvas** — used for event announcements only; not a place people spend time
- **iNaturalist** — where the action is, but it flies by too fast for most volunteers to notice
- **Ecdysis** — data CRUD only; no interaction or feeds
- **Facebook** — works passably but doesn't integrate with the data, and many people rightfully scorn it

There is a real unmet need for a platform that integrates the data AND fosters community around the Washington Bee Atlas project.

## How to Apply

When scoping new features, ask: does this help a first-time visitor understand what's here and do something useful? And does this bring collectors closer together around their shared work?
